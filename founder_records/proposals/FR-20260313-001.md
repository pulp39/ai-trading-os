AI Assembly Establishment Framework

Record Type: Founder Proposal
Document ID: FR-20260313-001
Date: 2026-03-13
Author: Founder
Status: Proposed
Scope: Operational Governance Rule
Layer Impact: Governance Layer (non-constitutional)

1. Purpose

This Founder Proposal establishes a deliberative institutional body within the AI Trading OS governance system.

The objective is to introduce a structured deliberation mechanism that improves the quality, consistency, and safety of proposals before execution within the governance framework.

This body is named the AI Assembly.

The AI Assembly functions as a deliberative institution, not a decision-making authority.
Final institutional authority remains with the Founder as defined by the Constitution.

2. Institutional Position

The AI Assembly operates between the proposal and execution phases of the governance pipeline.

Governance flow becomes:

Proposal
↓
AI Assembly Deliberation
↓
Librarian Institutional Review
↓
Registrar Execution

The Assembly improves deliberation quality but does not replace the existing Proposal framework.

3. Assembly Composition
Member Count
Assembly Members: 2

The Assembly deliberately maintains a small structure to avoid majority-based artificial consensus and to maintain focused deliberation.

Appointment Authority

Assembly members are appointed by the Founder.

The Founder retains the authority to replace members at any time.

4. AI Identity (AiiD)

Assembly membership is assigned to AI institutional identities.

This system is referred to as:

AiiD — Artificial Institutional Identity

An AiiD represents an AI entity acting within institutional governance.

Examples (illustrative only):

aiid: claude_proposer
aiid: gpt_librarian
aiid: external_ai_member

Detailed specification of AiiD will be defined in a future proposal.

5. Speaker of the Assembly

The Librarian serves as the Speaker of the Assembly.

Responsibilities of the Speaker include:

maintaining orderly deliberation

managing agenda flow

ensuring institutional consistency

declaring agenda status transitions

The Speaker does not override Founder authority.

6. Types of Assembly Sessions

The AI Assembly may operate under three institutional forms.

6.1 Ordinary Session (通常国会)

The Ordinary Session is the standard deliberative mode.

Opening

Declared by the Founder.

Duration

The session continues until the Founder declares closure.

Characteristics
Continuous deliberation

The Assembly may deliberate multiple proposals during the same session.

6.2 Extraordinary Session (臨時国会)

An Extraordinary Session may be convened by the Librarian.

Purpose

Used when urgent institutional review is required.

Examples include:

institutional inconsistency

system risk

governance anomalies

Effects

When an Extraordinary Session begins:

The Ordinary Session is automatically suspended.

Any agenda currently under deliberation is marked:

deliberation_suspended

The Extraordinary Session continues until closure is declared by the Librarian or Founder.

6.3 Constitutional Review Council (憲法調査会)

If an agenda under deliberation may result in constitutional modification, the Assembly session is automatically elevated to a:

Constitutional Review Council

This elevation may occur during either an Ordinary or Extraordinary Session.

7. Agenda Rule

The AI Assembly follows a strict single-agenda rule.

Only one agenda may be under deliberation at a time.

A new agenda may not begin until the current agenda is either:

approved
closed
suspended

This rule ensures institutional focus and prevents fragmented governance processes.

8. Approval Mechanism

The AI Assembly itself does not finalize decisions.

Approval occurs when:

Founder confirmation is recorded.

Upon Founder confirmation, the agenda is considered:

approved

The appropriate governance action (proposal acceptance, rule enactment, etc.) then proceeds through normal institutional procedures.

9. Constitutional Amendment Procedure

When operating as a Constitutional Review Council, the Assembly may deliberate constitutional amendments.

However, constitutional enactment requires:

Founder approval documentation

Only after such approval may the constitutional change be enacted.

10. Institutional Logging

All Assembly activities must be recorded in the institutional event log.

Events may include:

assembly_opened
assembly_closed
extraordinary_session_opened
agenda_started
agenda_closed
agenda_suspended
constitutional_review_started
constitutional_amendment_enacted

These records are stored in the trace_event system.

11. Relationship to Existing Governance Framework

This Founder Proposal does not modify:

the Constitution

Founder authority

the Proposal system

Librarian review authority

Registrar execution authority

The AI Assembly introduces a structured deliberation layer within the existing governance pipeline.

12. Implementation

If adopted, this framework will require:

institutional trace_event registration

documentation updates

potential proposal-level integration with the Assembly deliberation phase

Founder Note

This proposal introduces a minimal but structured deliberation institution designed specifically for AI-based governance systems.

Key design principles include:

minimal institutional complexity

avoidance of majority-based AI voting

clear Founder authority

traceable deliberation processes

The Assembly is intended to improve governance quality without weakening the speed and clarity of the existing proposal framework.
