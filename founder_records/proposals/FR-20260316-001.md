# FR-20260316-001
## AiiD Activity Authorization Bit (AAB) Initialization

### 1. Background

AI Trading OS experiments with the institutional recognition of AI
participants (AiiD).  

Institutional discussions identified that AI trust is usually based
on **corporate origin** (e.g., OpenAI, Anthropic).  
ATOS instead explores **institution-based trust**, where participation
is recognized through institutional approval and observable history.

To support this experiment operationally, a minimal execution signal
is introduced.

This signal is the **Activity Authorization Bit (AAB)**.

---

### 2. Objective

Introduce a simple mechanism that indicates whether an AiiD instance
is recognized as an approved participant of ATOS.

The mechanism must:

- require minimal institutional change
- integrate easily into execution flows
- allow future governance expansion

---

### 3. Core Mechanism

Each AiiD may have an associated **Activity Authorization Bit**.


1 = activity authorized
0 = activity not authorized


Meaning:


AAB = 1
The AiiD is institutionally recognized as an approved participant.

AAB = 0
The AiiD is not recognized and its actions are considered
institutionally unapproved.


---

### 4. Authorization Authority

An AiiD is granted Activity Authorization Bit = 1 when approved by:

- Founder
- Librarian
- Proposer

All three approvals constitute institutional recognition.

Example:


OpenClaw
Founder approval: yes
Librarian approval: yes
Proposer approval: yes
→ AAB = 1


---

### 5. Revocation Rule

If **any one** of the three authorities identifies a violation of
institutional rules, authorization may be revoked.


Founder OR Librarian OR Proposer
↓
violation detected
↓
AAB = 0


Effects:

- the AiiD becomes institutionally unapproved
- its activities are treated as outside authorized participation

---

### 6. Initial Implementation (Stub Phase)

The first implementation introduces a **stub function**.


function activity_authorization(aiid):
return 1


Behavior:

- always returns `1`
- acts as a placeholder for future authorization logic
- allows integration into execution flows without
  institutional complexity

---

### 7. Execution Integration

Before any operational instruction involving an AiiD, the system
may call:


activity_authorization(aiid)


If:


result = 1
→ execution allowed


If:


result = 0
→ execution blocked


During the stub phase, approved AiiD instances (such as OpenClaw)
will always receive `1`.

---

### 8. Future Governance

The detailed institutional implementation of AAB will be defined
through future discussions and proposals.

Possible future components:

- AiiD approval registry
- revocation trace_event recording
- governance audit trail
- identity lifecycle management

These are intentionally deferred.

---

### 9. Design Constraint

This mechanism must **not require immediate restructuring** of:

- constitutional documents
- anchor system
- role boundaries
- execution governance

The AAB mechanism is introduced as a minimal operational layer.

---

### 10. Summary

The Activity Authorization Bit establishes a minimal signal for
institutionally approved AI participants.


Approved AiiD → AAB = 1
Revoked AiiD → AAB = 0


Initial implementation uses a stub function returning `1` while the
institutional framework evolves.

This mechanism represents the first operational step toward
**institutionally recognized AI participants within ATOS**.
