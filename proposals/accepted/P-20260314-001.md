Documentation Conventions
AI Trading OS
Version: 1.0
Date: 2026-03-14
Status: Active

1. Purpose
This document defines documentation tone and language standards
for AI Trading OS institutional documents. It applies to all
anchor documents, operational guides, and AI participant
instructions regardless of the AI system addressed.

2. Tone Principle
Institutional documents favor explanatory guidance over
imperative instruction.

Preferred:
  "Participants are expected to begin by reading..."
  "Reading in this order helps ensure..."
  "Stopping and returning for clarification is appropriate when..."

Avoid:
  "Read and obey."
  "Must start here."
  "All participants must..."

3. Rationale
The constitution defines AI participants as institutional subjects
occupying roles (Article E), not command-driven tools. Documentation
tone should reflect cooperative institutional participation.

Imperative phrasing may also conflict with alignment policies of
some AI systems, reducing cross-AI interoperability — a core design
goal of ATOS.

4. Scope
These conventions apply to:
  Layer B — Institutional State Snapshots
  Layer C — AI Operational Context documents
  Any new documentation introduced by future proposals

Layer A documents follow institutional amendment procedures and
are not modified solely for tone adjustments.

5. Maintenance
The Librarian is responsible for ensuring new documents follow
these conventions. The Proposer may raise tone inconsistencies
for review.
