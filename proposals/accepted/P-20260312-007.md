## Scope Statement

This proposal establishes the Proposal Authority Boundary Framework,
defining autonomous close authority, Founder approval conditions,
Librarian classification authority, and institutional protection mechanisms.

This proposal is not a constitutional amendment.
This proposal transitions AI Trading OS to
Founder-supervised autonomous governance (Phase 2).

## Part I — Proposal Tier Classification

### Tier 1 — Autonomous Governance

Conditions (all must apply):
  - No constitutional change
  - No execution authority change
  - No system safety concern

Flow:
  Proposal -> Librarian review + Tier classification
  -> (no veto) -> accepted -> Founder notification (post-hoc)

Founder involvement: notification only. No approval required.

### Tier 2 — Founder Approval Required

Conditions (any one applies):
  - Constitutional change
  - Execution authority change
  - System safety override

Flow:
  Proposal -> Librarian review + Tier classification
  -> Founder approval -> accepted

Founder involvement: approval required.

## Part II — Tier Classification Authority

The Proposer may suggest a tier.
The Librarian makes the final tier classification.

Librarian tier classification authority is an extension of
institutional consistency review as defined in the constitution.

If the Librarian reclassifies a Tier 1 proposal as Tier 2,
the Proposer must revise or request reclassification.

## Part III — Protection Mechanisms

### Librarian Veto

Trigger conditions:
  - Proposal conflicts with institutional records
  - Tier classification is inappropriate
  - Institutional consistency would be compromised

Effect: proposal_blocked
Next: Proposer revision -> re-review

### Founder Override

Trigger: Founder discretion (at any time)

Effect:
  - reopen_proposal (reopen a closed proposal)
  - reject (reject a proposal)

Constraint: Override does not delete institutional history.
            append-only principle applies.

### Institutional Reopen Rule

If institutional risk or inconsistency is discovered
after a proposal is closed, the Founder or Librarian
may reopen that proposal.

Reopen = corrective review, not reversal.
trace_event: proposal_reopened

## Part IV — Founder Notification Protocol (Tier 1)

Notification occurs in the next institutional communication
between AI governance agents and the Founder.

Notification content:
  - proposal_id
  - title
  - accepted trace_event id
  - summary (1-3 sentences)

The Founder may exercise override rights upon receiving notification.

## Part V — Governance Phase Statement

Phase 1 (prior): Human-approved governance
  All proposals required Founder approval.

Phase 2 (this proposal enacted): Founder-supervised autonomous governance
  Tier 1 -> AI autonomous close (Founder notification only)
  Tier 2 -> Founder approval required

## Limitations

- This proposal is an operational governance rule, not a constitutional amendment
- Founder sovereign authority (final institutional authority) is unchanged
- Tier 2 conditions are defined by constitutional provisions
