Background
On 2026-03-17, ATOS established a continuous multi-symbol market observation pipeline via kabuStation. Observations are currently recorded in research.trace_event as event logs.
This proposal introduces public.board_snapshots as the canonical structured persistence layer for market observations, enabling the transition from observation logging to interpretation-ready data.

Core Distinction（制度的定義）
trace_event = 履歴（narrative record of institutional events）
snapshots   = 状態（structured factual market state）
These two objects serve different institutional purposes and must remain distinct:

trace_event records that something happened (institutional narrative)
board_snapshots records what the market state was (factual observation)

This separation directly implements constitution Article 3 (Institutional Objects) and Article 5 (Role Boundary Principle): Collectors record facts; Proposers interpret them.

Change 1 — board_snapshots as Canonical Observation Store
Current state:
Market observations are written to research.trace_event with event_type: market_observation.
Proposed state:
Market observations are written to public.board_snapshots as the primary store. A corresponding trace_event entry records the institutional act of observation.
kabuStation board data
↓
public.board_snapshots（structured market state）
↓
research.trace_event（record of observation act）
Schema reference (confirmed in DB_STATUS_ANCHOR):
sqlpublic.board_snapshots:
  id, captured_at, symbol, exchange,
  current_price, bid_price, ask_price,
  bid_qty, ask_qty, trading_volume,
  vwap, payload (jsonb)
```

---

### Change 2 — Observation to Snapshot Conversion Rule

Each board fetch from kabuStation produces one snapshot record per symbol:
```
symbol        ← board response symbol field
exchange      ← board response exchange field
current_price, bid_price, ask_price ← normalized fields
payload       ← full raw board response (jsonb)
captured_at   ← timestamp of fetch (JST)
The payload field preserves the complete raw observation, ensuring full traceability.

Change 3 — trace_event Relationship
For each Collector run, one trace_event entry is recorded:
json{

  "actor": "OpenClaw",

  "symbols": ["7203", "8306", "6758"],
  "snapshot_count": 3,
  "aab_bundle_id": "AAB-YYYYMMDD-NNN"
}
```

### Change 4 — OpenClaw Execution Scope

Under this proposal, OpenClaw's Collector execution scope includes:
```
✅ board fetch from kabuStation
✅ INSERT into public.board_snapshots
✅ trace_event recording (collector_run)
✅ AAB start/end recording

❌ hypothesis generation (→ Proposer)
❌ institutional interpretation (→ Librarian)
❌ governance decisions (→ Founder)

Change 5 — Minimum AAB Definition
json{
  "event_type": "aab_start",
  "bundle_id": "AAB-20260317-001",
  "actor": "OpenClaw",
  "scope": "collector_run",
  "symbols": ["7203", "8306", "6758"]
}
AAB entries are recorded in research.trace_event as a subtype. No new table required at this stage.

Change 6 — Snapshot Idempotency
To prevent duplicate inserts, the following constraint is recommended:
sqlUNIQUE(symbol, captured_at)
If duplicate insert is attempted:
sqlON CONFLICT (symbol, captured_at) DO NOTHING
```

Rationale: Collector may retry or re-run within the same timestamp window. Idempotency ensures institutional record integrity.

---

### Change 7 — Time Standardization

All timestamps must follow:
```
captured_at : JST (Asia/Tokyo) — market-observed time
DB internal (ts) : UTC — database standard
Conversion rule: captured_at is the market-observed time and must not be converted. It represents the actual moment of market observation and must remain consistent with Tokyo Stock Exchange trading hours.
Rationale: Market interpretation requires local market time consistency. Mixing timezones in captured_at would corrupt any time-series analysis.

Change 8 — Payload Role
The payload field serves as:

Full raw market data archive
Source of truth for reconstruction
Future re-processing compatibility

No field in payload should be discarded or transformed destructively. Even if normalized fields are added in future schema revisions, the raw payload must be preserved.
