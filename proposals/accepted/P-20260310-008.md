## Scope Statement

This proposal requests the formal admission of the institution's first Collector agent, designated `base_collector_v1`, into AI Trading OS under the Collector role framework established in P-20260310-007.

The proposal covers:

- Candidate declaration including agent identity, type, and data source description
- Requested scope authorization
- Provisional identifier assignment request to the Librarian
- Trial observation recording requirements including trace_event labeling
- Trial failure handling and provisional identifier retirement
- Boundary definition between trial and formal observation operations
- Cadence deviation disclosure procedure
- Declaration of role boundary acceptance

Admission is subject to Librarian review, trial observation assessment, and Founder approval per the procedure defined in P-20260310-007.

---

## Purpose

The institution's observation layer is fully specified across proposals P-20260310-003 through P-20260310-007. The hypothesis formation framework is established in P-20260310-006. The Collector role definition and admissibility framework are established in P-20260310-007.

The institution is now structurally ready to begin observation operations. The only remaining prerequisite is the formal admission of at least one authorized Collector agent. This proposal initiates that admission.

The requested scope is deliberately narrow. Equity index futures at daily resolution is the asset class and timeframe for which the institution has a fully specified parameter set (P-20260310-004). Beginning with the narrowest operationally defined scope reduces the risk of conformance failure during the trial period and allows the institution to validate the full observation pipeline before expanding scope.

Instrument specification is deferred to the Librarian's registry entry rather than fixed in this proposal, allowing the Librarian to apply operational judgment about which specific instruments are most appropriate for the first observation cycle.

## Part IV — Provisional Identifier Assignment Request

Per the Librarian's editorial clarification to P-20260310-007, trial observation records must use a provisional Collector identifier assigned by the Librarian so that trial records remain traceable even if admission is ultimately rejected.

This proposal formally requests that the Librarian assign a provisional identifier to `base_collector_v1` for use during the trial observation period. The recommended provisional identifier format is:

`PROV-base_collector_v1-[YYYYMMDD]`

where `[YYYYMMDD]` reflects the date the provisional identifier is assigned.

The provisional identifier:

- Is used exclusively in the `actor` field of trial observation trace_event entries
- Is registered in the Collector registry under provisional status from the date of assignment
- Remains active until Founder approval of full admission, at which point it is retired and replaced by the permanent identifier
- Remains active in the registry as a historical record if admission is rejected, per Part VI below

## Part V — Trial Observation Protocol

### 5.1 Trial Observation Requirement

Per P-20260310-007 Section 2.2 Step 2, `base_collector_v1` must produce a minimum of three trial observation payload records under provisional status before full admission is considered.

### 5.2 Trial Observation Trace_Event Recording Requirement

All trial observation records must be explicitly recorded in the trace_event system using the provisional identifier assigned in Part IV. Each trial observation trace_event entry must carry the following additional field to label it as a trial record:

```
observation_status: trial
provisional_collector_id: [assigned provisional identifier]
```

This labeling ensures that:

- Trial records are traceable in the institutional record under the provisional identifier
- Trial records are clearly distinguishable from formally admitted observation records
- The Librarian can review trial records by querying the `observation_status: trial` field
- Trial records remain in the trace_event history as a permanent record of the admission process regardless of its outcome

Trial records carry `observation_status: trial` for the duration of the provisional period. Upon Founder approval of full admission, subsequent records produced by the same agent carry `observation_status: formal` and use the permanent institutional identifier.

### 5.3 Trial Observation Standards

Trial observations must conform to all institutional standards as if they were fully admitted records:

- Payload structure per P-20260310-003
- Parameter specification per P-20260310-004
- Signal quality classification per P-20260310-005
- Traceability identifiers per P-20260310-006
- Cadence deviation disclosure per Librarian clarification to P-20260310-007

Trial records are not admissible as hypothesis formation inputs until full admission is granted. They are institutional records of the admission process, not operational observation records.

### 5.4 Trial Observation Review

The Librarian reviews trial records per P-20260310-007 Section 2.2 Step 3, assessing:

- Structural conformance with P-20260310-003
- Parameter conformance with P-20260310-004
- Quality classification conformance with P-20260310-005
- Traceability identifier presence and accuracy
- Appropriate reliability notes disclosure
- Correct use of `observation_status: trial` labeling and provisional identifier
- Absence of interpretive content

If the trial records reveal systematic conformance failures, the Librarian may request additional trial records or recommend rejection of this admission proposal to the Founder.

## Part VI — Trial Failure Handling and Provisional Identifier Retirement

### 6.1 Trial Failure Definition

A trial is considered to have failed if:

- The Librarian determines that the trial observation records demonstrate systematic conformance failures that cannot be remediated through clarification or a small number of additional trial records
- The Librarian recommends rejection of the admission proposal to the Founder
- The Founder rejects the admission proposal

### 6.2 Provisional Identifier Retirement Upon Rejection

If admission is rejected, the following institutional actions are taken:

- The provisional identifier is retired from active use and its status in the Collector registry is updated to `rejected`
- The registry entry retains the provisional identifier, the date of assignment, the date of rejection, and a summary of the grounds for rejection
- A trace_event entry is recorded confirming the rejection and the retirement of the provisional identifier
- All trial observation records produced under the provisional identifier remain in the trace_event history with their `observation_status: trial` labeling intact, providing a permanent record of the admission attempt

Retired provisional identifiers are never reused or reassigned to a different agent. If `base_collector_v1` seeks re-admission following rejection, the prior rejection must be disclosed in the re-admission proposal per P-20260310-007 Section 3.4, and a new provisional identifier will be assigned for the new trial period.

### 6.3 Partial Trial Completion

If the trial period is abandoned before the minimum of three trial records is produced — for any reason other than successful admission — the provisional identifier is retired under the same procedure as a rejection. Partial trial records remain in the trace_event history and the incomplete admission attempt is recorded in the Collector registry.

## Part VII — Boundary Between Trial and Formal Observation Operations

### 7.1 Transition Trigger

The boundary between trial observation and formal observation operations is defined by a single institutional event: Founder approval of this admission proposal following positive Librarian review of the trial records.

No other condition — including the passage of time, the production of additional trial records beyond the minimum three, or informal Librarian assessment — constitutes activation of formal observation status.

### 7.2 Pre-Transition State

During the trial period, `base_collector_v1` operates as follows:

- Uses the provisional identifier in all trace_event entries
- Labels all observation records with `observation_status: trial`
- Produces records conforming to all institutional standards
- Does not contribute admissible records to the hypothesis formation input stream
- Is subject to provisional status in the Collector registry

### 7.3 Transition Actions

Upon Founder approval, the following transition actions are executed by the Librarian:

1. The permanent institutional identifier is assigned to `base_collector_v1`
2. The provisional identifier is retired in the Collector registry (status updated to `superseded`)
3. The Collector registry entry is updated to `active` status with the permanent identifier and the activation date
4. A trace_event entry of type `collector_activated` is recorded using the permanent identifier, confirming the transition to formal observation status
5. The admission proposal is archived in the accepted proposals directory

### 7.4 Post-Transition State

From the activation date forward, `base_collector_v1` operates as follows:

- Uses the permanent institutional identifier in all trace_event entries
- Labels all observation records with `observation_status: formal`
- Produces records that are admissible as hypothesis formation inputs subject to the criteria defined in P-20260310-007 Part IV
- Is subject to active status in the Collector registry and all associated obligations, including the suspension and revocation conditions of P-20260310-007 Part III

### 7.5 Inadmissibility of Trial Records After Transition

Trial records produced under the provisional identifier do not become admissible as hypothesis formation inputs upon transition to formal status. Their `observation_status: trial` classification is permanent. Only records produced under the permanent identifier after the activation date carry `observation_status: formal` and are eligible for admissibility assessment under P-20260310-007 Part IV.

This boundary ensures that the hypothesis formation layer operates exclusively on records produced under formally authorized and fully reviewed institutional conditions.

## Part VIII — Cadence Deviation Disclosure Procedure

Per the Librarian's editorial clarification to P-20260310-007, temporary deviations from the declared observation cadence must be disclosed.

`base_collector_v1` adopts the following cadence deviation disclosure procedure:

- If an observation record cannot be produced within the defined post-session window due to data unavailability, the Collector records a trace_event entry of type `observation_cadence_deviation` documenting the affected session, the reason for deviation, and the expected resolution
- If the deviation extends beyond one session, a subsequent trace_event entry confirms either resumption of normal cadence or continuation of the deviation condition
- During the trial period, cadence deviation entries use the provisional identifier
- During formal operation, cadence deviation entries use the permanent identifier
- Cadence deviations do not affect the admissibility of observation records produced in adjacent sessions, provided those records satisfy all seven admissibility criteria of P-20260310-007

`base_collector_v1` declares acceptance of the following role boundaries as defined in P-20260310-007:
