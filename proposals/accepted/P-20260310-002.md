Title

Foundational Research Direction — Observation Schema Design as Recommended Initial Research Direction

Status

accepted

Scope Statement

This proposal is scoped to a single recommendation:

Observation schema design should be treated as the recommended initial research direction of AI Trading OS.

This proposal does not define the schema itself. It does not address signal quality classification or hypothesis cataloging. Those directions are noted in the Future Proposal Directions section as downstream work to be pursued after schema design produces an initial structured observation layer.

Purpose

P-20260310-001 confirmed that the institutional proposal pipeline is operational. The system is now ready for substantive research activity.

Before the system can generate high-quality research hypotheses, it requires a stable and shared vocabulary for recording market observations. This proposal argues that defining that vocabulary — through a structured trace_event observation schema — is the correct first investment for the research layer.

Research Hypothesis

If AI Trading OS defines and adopts a structured schema for recording market observations, then the quality and comparability of trace_event records will be sufficient to support systematic AI hypothesis generation.

Without a defined schema, trace_event records may vary in structure, completeness, and interpretive depth. This variation silently constrains the Proposer layer without producing any visible institutional failure. A schema addresses this constraint at the source.

Context and Motivation

The constitution establishes traceability and knowledge accumulation as core institutional principles. The research process documentation defines the trace_event as the foundational input to the proposal layer. However, neither document specifies what dimensions a market-oriented trace_event should capture.

This gap is the subject of this proposal.

A trace_event that records only that a market event occurred is less useful than one that records the event within a defined context — price level, volume, volatility regime, and relevant macro conditions. The difference between these two records is not a matter of interpretation (which belongs to the Proposer layer) but of observational completeness (which belongs to the Collector layer).

Defining what completeness means for this institution is the research question this proposal recommends addressing first.

Primary Research Direction
Observation Schema Design

Research question:
What is the minimum sufficient observation schema for market-oriented trace_event records to enable meaningful AI hypothesis generation?

Scope:
The schema should define the observational dimensions that Collectors are expected to capture when recording market-relevant events. Candidate dimensions include, but are not limited to:

price level and recent directional context

volume relative to a defined historical baseline

volatility regime (realized and, where available, implied)

proximity to scheduled macro events

asset correlation state relative to a defined reference period

Expected institutional outcome:
A schema definition reduces observation variance across trace_event records, increases the comparability of observations over time, and provides the Proposer layer with a stable input structure for hypothesis generation.

Form of output:
The research direction proposed here should result in a subsequent proposal — drafted by the Proposer and reviewed by the Librarian — that contains a candidate schema definition for Founder consideration.

Confidence Statement

Confidence level: Moderate-High

The claim that observation structure improves downstream hypothesis quality is grounded in general scientific methodology and is treated here as a founding institutional assumption. Whether any specific schema definition achieves this goal remains an open research question to be addressed in subsequent proposals.

Limitations

This proposal does not define a schema. Schema definition is the subject of a subsequent proposal.

This proposal does not evaluate specific markets, instruments, or asset classes. Those choices are downstream of schema design.

The hypothesis that schema-compliant observations improve hypothesis quality is an institutional assumption at this stage, not yet an empirically validated finding within this system.

This proposal is advisory. It does not authorize any execution, data collection, or system modification.

Future Proposal Directions

The following research directions are outside the scope of this proposal. They are recorded here to support institutional continuity and to signal the expected research sequence.

Signal quality classification
Once a schema is adopted, a subsequent proposal may address whether trace_event records should carry a structural quality flag indicating observational completeness. This flag would be set by Collectors based on data availability, not interpretive judgment.

Baseline hypothesis catalog
Once structured observations are being recorded, a subsequent proposal may define a catalog of foundational hypothesis classes for the institution to investigate systematically (e.g., momentum persistence, volatility regime transitions, correlation stability).

These directions are not part of this proposal. They are anticipated downstream work.
