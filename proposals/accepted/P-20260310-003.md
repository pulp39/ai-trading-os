# P-20260310-003

## Title

Candidate Observation Payload Structure for Market-Oriented trace_event Records

## Status

draft (revised)

## Proposal Type

research_hypothesis / schema_direction

---

## Scope Statement

This proposal responds directly to the research direction established by P-20260310-002. That proposal identified observation schema design as the institution's first research priority. This proposal now advances that direction by presenting a candidate structured observation payload intended to be embedded within market-oriented trace_event records.

This proposal does not redefine or modify the core trace_event structure. The trace_event remains the general institutional event log. This proposal concerns only the structured content carried within the observation payload field of market-oriented trace_event entries.

This proposal is advisory. It does not implement the payload structure. It does not modify the database. It does not alter the trace_event recording infrastructure. It presents a research hypothesis about what a minimum sufficient observation payload should contain, and invites institutional challenge and refinement.

---

## Structural Clarification: trace_event and Observation Payload

The trace_event is the institutional container for all recorded events, including market observations. Its core structure — event type, timestamp, actor, and content — remains unchanged and is not subject to this proposal.

Market-oriented trace_event records carry a structured observation payload within their content field. This payload is what this proposal addresses. The relationship is as follows:

trace_event
├── event_type: market_observation
├── timestamp: [UTC]
├── actor: [collector identifier]
└── observation_payload:
└── [structured market observation — defined by this proposal]


The observation payload is embedded within the trace_event. It extends the informational depth of market-oriented records without altering the trace_event structure that governs all institutional events.

---

## Purpose

The institutional trace_event layer currently serves as factual observational memory. Trace_events are recorded without interpretation. This separation between observation and interpretation is a constitutional requirement and must be preserved.

However, the current trace_event layer has been developed primarily in the context of institutional events — pipeline initializations, documentation commits, constitutional enactments. These are well-served by a general event description field.

Market-oriented observations present a different challenge. A market observation that records only that a price movement occurred is less institutionally useful than one that captures the conditions surrounding that movement in a consistent, comparable form. Without a defined observation payload structure, market trace_events will vary in completeness and comparability across Collectors and over time, reducing their value as research inputs.

This proposal offers a candidate observation payload structure to address that gap.

---

## Research Hypothesis

> A market-oriented trace_event observation payload that captures five core dimensions — price context, volume context, volatility regime, macro event proximity, and correlation state — will provide sufficient structured input for the Proposer layer to generate testable, revisable research hypotheses.

The hypothesis implies a corollary: that omitting any one of these five dimensions meaningfully degrades hypothesis quality in a way that can be identified and measured over successive proposal cycles.

Both claims are treated as open research questions at this stage. This proposal does not assert they are proven. It asserts they are the right questions to investigate first.

---

## Candidate Observation Payload

The following defines five dimensions for the structured observation payload embedded within market-oriented trace_event records. Each dimension specifies what the Collector should record, at what level of granularity, and why it is included.

This payload structure is proposed for a single-asset, single-timeframe observation context. Multi-asset and multi-timeframe extensions are out of scope for this proposal.

---

### Dimension 1 — Price Context

**What to record:**

- Current price level (absolute)
- Direction of recent movement: one of `up`, `down`, `flat`
- Magnitude of recent movement relative to a defined lookback window (e.g., percentage change over the prior N periods)
- Whether the current price is above or below a defined reference level (e.g., rolling mean, prior session close)

**Why included:**

Price is the primary observable in most market contexts. Direction and magnitude allow the Proposer layer to reason about momentum and mean-reversion dynamics without requiring the Collector to interpret those dynamics.

**Collector constraint:**

The Collector records these values factually. The Collector does not classify the movement as significant or insignificant. That judgment belongs to the Proposer.

---

### Dimension 2 — Volume Context

**What to record:**

- Current period volume (absolute)
- Volume relative to a defined historical baseline (e.g., ratio to N-period average): expressed as a numeric ratio, not a qualitative label
- Whether the observation period is within normal market hours or outside them

**Why included:**

Volume contextualizes price movement. A price change on high relative volume carries different research implications than the same change on low relative volume. Recording the ratio rather than a label preserves factual neutrality while enabling meaningful interpretation.

**Collector constraint:**

The Collector records the ratio. The Collector does not assess whether volume is significant. That assessment belongs to the Proposer.

---

### Dimension 3 — Volatility Regime

**What to record:**

- Realized volatility over a defined short lookback window (e.g., 5-period)
- Realized volatility over a defined longer lookback window (e.g., 20-period)
- Ratio of short-window to long-window realized volatility
- Where available: implied volatility at the time of observation

**Why included:**

Volatility regime is a primary conditioning variable for market behavior. Many hypotheses about price dynamics are regime-dependent. Recording both short and long realized volatility, and their ratio, allows the Proposer layer to reason about whether the market is in an expanding or contracting volatility environment without requiring the Collector to make that determination.

**Collector constraint:**

All values are numeric. The Collector does not classify the regime. Regime classification belongs to the Proposer.

---

### Dimension 4 — Macro Event Proximity

**What to record:**

- Whether a scheduled macro event (e.g., central bank announcement, major economic release) is within a defined forward window (e.g., 24 hours): boolean
- Whether a scheduled macro event occurred within a defined backward window (e.g., 24 hours): boolean
- Event type label if applicable (e.g., `central_bank`, `employment`, `inflation`, `none`)

**Why included:**

Market behavior frequently exhibits pre-event and post-event dynamics that are structurally different from baseline conditions. Recording proximity to scheduled events allows the Proposer layer to condition hypotheses on the event cycle without the Collector needing to interpret the event's likely impact.

**Collector constraint:**

The Collector records proximity and event type from a defined event calendar. The Collector does not assess the event's relevance or expected impact.

---

### Dimension 5 — Correlation State

**What to record:**

- Rolling pairwise correlation between the observed asset and a defined reference asset or index over a defined lookback window (e.g., 20-period)
- Direction of recent correlation change: one of `increasing`, `decreasing`, `stable`

**Why included:**

Asset behavior is rarely isolated. Correlation state conditions the interpretation of price and volume observations. A price decline in a high-correlation environment has different research implications than the same decline in a low-correlation environment. Recording correlation state allows the Proposer layer to reason about systemic versus idiosyncratic dynamics.

**Collector constraint:**

The Collector records the numeric correlation value and the directional change. The Collector does not interpret whether the correlation level is unusual or meaningful.

## Proposed Observation Payload Structure

The following illustrates how the five dimensions would be represented as a structured observation payload embedded within a market-oriented trace_event record. This is a research illustration, not an implementation specification.

trace_event:
event_type: market_observation
timestamp: [UTC]
actor: [collector identifier]
observation_payload:
asset: [asset identifier]
timeframe: [observation period, e.g., 1h, 1d]

price_context:
  level: [numeric]
  direction: [up | down | flat]
  magnitude_pct: [numeric]
  vs_reference: [above | below | at]

volume_context:
  absolute: [numeric]
  ratio_to_baseline: [numeric]
  session_type: [regular | extended | off_hours]

volatility_context:
  realized_short: [numeric]
  realized_long: [numeric]
  ratio_short_to_long: [numeric]
  implied: [numeric | null]

macro_proximity:
  event_upcoming: [true | false]
  event_recent: [true | false]
  event_type: [central_bank | employment | inflation | other | none]

correlation_state:
  reference_asset: [asset identifier]
  rolling_correlation: [numeric]
  direction: [increasing | decreasing | stable]

  
The trace_event container (event_type, timestamp, actor) is unchanged. The observation_payload field carries all market-specific structured content.

---

## Minimum Sufficient Payload Hypothesis

The five dimensions above are proposed as the minimum sufficient set for the following reason: each dimension captures a class of market information that is not derivable from the others. Removing any one dimension creates an observational blind spot that the Proposer layer cannot compensate for through interpretation alone.

This claim is itself a research hypothesis and should be treated as such. The Librarian or subsequent research cycles may identify additional dimensions that are non-redundant, or may find that certain dimensions are redundant in practice and can be collapsed.

---

## Open Research Questions

1. What lookback window lengths are appropriate for each dimension, and should they be fixed or adaptive?
2. How should the payload handle missing data (e.g., implied volatility unavailable for certain instruments)?
3. Should the payload structure be asset-class-specific, or can a single structure serve equity, fixed income, and commodity observations?
4. How should payload completeness be verified across Collectors, and what constitutes a structurally incomplete observation?

## Confidence Statement

**Confidence level:** Moderate

The five-dimension structure is grounded in standard market microstructure and quantitative research practice. The claim that these five dimensions are jointly sufficient and individually necessary is a hypothesis, not an established finding. It is offered as a starting point for institutional debate rather than a conclusion.

---

## Limitations

- This proposal does not implement an observation payload in any database or recording system.
- This proposal does not modify the core trace_event structure.
- This proposal does not specify which assets, instruments, or markets the payload should be applied to first.
- The lookback window parameters referenced throughout are illustrative placeholders.
- This proposal is advisory. It does not authorize any execution, data collection, or system modification.
