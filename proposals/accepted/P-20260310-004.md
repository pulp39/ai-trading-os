# P-20260310-004

## Title

Candidate Parameter Values for the First Operational Observation Payload — Equity Index Futures, Daily Timeframe

## Status

draft

## Proposal Type

research_hypothesis / parameter_specification

---

## Scope Statement

This proposal defines candidate parameter values for the five-dimension observation payload established in P-20260310-003, scoped to a single asset class and a single observation timeframe:

- **Asset class**: equity index futures
- **Observation timeframe**: daily (1D)

The choice of asset class and timeframe is itself a research hypothesis and is argued below. All parameter values presented are candidates for institutional debate, not implementation directives.

This proposal does not implement the payload. It does not modify the database or recording infrastructure. It does not alter institutional governance.

---

## Rationale for Asset Class and Timeframe Selection

### Equity index futures

Equity index futures are proposed as the first operational context for the following reasons:

1. **Data availability**: Major equity index futures (e.g., S&P 500, Nikkei 225) have long, continuous, and well-documented price and volume histories, supporting baseline period calculations.
2. **Liquidity and regularity**: These instruments trade with high liquidity during regular sessions, reducing the frequency of missing-data conditions in the payload.
3. **Macro event sensitivity**: Equity index futures are reliably responsive to the scheduled macro events defined in Dimension 4 of the payload, making that dimension immediately useful rather than structurally empty.
4. **Correlation reference availability**: Major equity indices have well-established cross-asset correlation relationships, making Dimension 5 parameter selection tractable.

### Daily timeframe

The daily timeframe is proposed as the first operational resolution for the following reasons:

1. **Signal-to-noise ratio**: Daily observations reduce the intraday noise that would complicate early hypothesis generation and payload validation.
2. **Macro event alignment**: Scheduled macro events are most naturally interpreted at daily resolution. A 24-hour event proximity window aligns cleanly with a daily observation cycle.
3. **Baseline period tractability**: Lookback windows expressed in days correspond to familiar market periods (1 week, 1 month, 1 quarter), making parameter choices interpretable and auditable.
4. **Institutional simplicity**: For the first operational payload cycle, a daily timeframe reduces the complexity of the Collector role and allows the research focus to remain on payload structure rather than on data pipeline throughput.

## Candidate Parameter Values by Dimension

---

### Dimension 1 — Price Context

| Parameter | Candidate Value | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Recent movement lookback window | 5 trading days | Captures one week of directional context; short enough to reflect current conditions, long enough to reduce single-day noise |
| Reference level for above/below comparison | 20-day simple moving average | Standard medium-term trend reference; widely used and interpretable across research contexts |
| Magnitude expression | Percentage change over 5-day window | Normalizes across instruments with different absolute price levels |

**Hypothesis**: A 5-day lookback captures the dominant short-term directional signal for equity index futures at daily resolution without overfitting to single-session moves. The 20-day moving average provides a stable reference level that changes slowly enough to serve as a meaningful trend anchor.

---

### Dimension 2 — Volume Context

| Parameter | Candidate Value | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Baseline period for volume ratio | 20 trading days | One calendar month; captures recent seasonal and liquidity patterns without extending into structurally different market regimes |
| Baseline calculation method | Simple average of daily volume over prior 20 sessions | Simple and auditable; avoids complexity in the Collector layer |
| Session type classification threshold | Regular hours only for ratio calculation | Extended-hours volume in futures can be structurally different; isolating regular session volume improves baseline comparability |

**Hypothesis**: A 20-day volume baseline captures the current liquidity regime for equity index futures without incorporating volume from materially different market conditions. Restricting the baseline to regular session hours reduces structural noise in the ratio.

---

### Dimension 3 — Volatility Regime

| Parameter | Candidate Value | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Short realized volatility window | 5 trading days | Matches the price context lookback; captures current volatility conditions |
| Long realized volatility window | 20 trading days | Provides a stable regime reference comparable to the volume baseline period |
| Realized volatility calculation method | Close-to-close standard deviation of daily log returns, annualized | Standard, reproducible, and well-documented method for equity instruments |
| Implied volatility source | Front-month at-the-money options on the corresponding index, where available | Most liquid and representative implied volatility measure for major equity index futures |

**Hypothesis**: The ratio of 5-day to 20-day realized volatility captures whether the current volatility environment is expanding or contracting relative to the recent regime. A ratio above 1.0 indicates rising short-term volatility; below 1.0 indicates compression. This ratio does not require the Collector to classify the regime — it provides a numeric input the Proposer layer can interpret.

---

### Dimension 4 — Macro Event Proximity

| Parameter | Candidate Value | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Forward event window | 1 trading day (next session) | At daily resolution, "upcoming" most naturally means the following session |
| Backward event window | 1 trading day (prior session) | Captures post-event dynamics that may persist into the current observation day |
| Event calendar source | Publicly available economic release calendars (e.g., central bank meeting schedules, non-farm payroll dates, CPI release dates) | Standardized, auditable, and reproducible |
| Event types to track | `central_bank`, `employment`, `inflation`, `gdp`, `none` | Covers the four macro release categories most consistently associated with equity index volatility regime shifts |

**Hypothesis**: For equity index futures at daily resolution, a one-session forward and backward window captures the majority of event-proximity effects. The four event type categories cover the scheduled releases most likely to condition market behavior in ways that would affect hypothesis generation.

---

### Dimension 5 — Correlation State

| Parameter | Candidate Value | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Reference asset | A major cross-regional equity index futures contract (e.g., if the primary observation asset is a US equity index future, the reference asset is a European or Asian equivalent) | Cross-regional correlation captures systemic versus regional dynamics without introducing currency or asset-class basis complications |
| Correlation lookback window | 20 trading days | Matches the volatility and volume baseline periods; provides a consistent regime reference across all dimensions |
| Correlation calculation method | Pearson correlation of daily log returns over the prior 20 sessions | Standard, reproducible, and symmetric |
| Direction classification thresholds | `increasing`: current 5-day correlation > prior 5-day correlation by more than 0.05; `decreasing`: difference < -0.05; `stable`: within ±0.05 | Threshold is a candidate value; 0.05 is proposed as a starting point that avoids noise-driven direction changes while remaining sensitive to meaningful shifts |

**Hypothesis**: A 20-day rolling correlation between two major equity index futures from different regions captures whether the global equity regime is in a risk-on synchronization phase or a regional divergence phase. This conditioning variable is expected to improve hypothesis quality for event-driven and momentum hypotheses.

## Internal Consistency Note

The candidate values share a common internal structure: a 5-day short window and a 20-day long window appear across price, volatility, and correlation dimensions. This alignment is intentional. It allows the Proposer layer to compare short-term and medium-term signals across dimensions using a consistent temporal reference. Whether this alignment produces better hypotheses than dimension-specific window choices is itself an open research question for future proposal cycles.

---

## Open Research Questions

1. Should the 5-day and 20-day window lengths be treated as fixed institutional parameters or as variables to be explored in subsequent research cycles?
2. Is the ±0.05 correlation direction threshold appropriate, or should it be derived empirically from historical correlation volatility for the chosen instrument pair?
3. For the reference asset in Dimension 5, should the choice be fixed institutionally or allowed to vary by primary observation asset?
4. How should the payload handle non-trading days and session gaps (e.g., public holidays in one region but not another)?
5. Should implied volatility be treated as a required or optional payload field, given its unavailability for some instruments?

## Confidence Statement

**Confidence level**: Moderate

The candidate values are grounded in standard quantitative market research practice and are internally consistent. The 5-day and 20-day window lengths are widely used conventions in equity market analysis, which supports their use as a starting point. However, their optimality for this institution's specific research purposes is unproven and should be treated as an institutional assumption to be validated through future observation and proposal cycles.

The correlation direction threshold of ±0.05 is the parameter with the lowest confidence. It is proposed as a reasonable starting point but may require empirical calibration once observation data is available.

## Limitations

- All parameter values are candidates. None are validated against historical data within this institution.
- This proposal covers one asset class and one timeframe. Extensions to other asset classes and timeframes are deferred to future proposals.
- The proposal does not address how parameter values should be reviewed or updated over time as market conditions evolve.
- This proposal is advisory. It does not authorize any execution, data collection, or system modification.
