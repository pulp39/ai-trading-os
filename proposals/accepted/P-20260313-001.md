## 1. Research Hypothesis

Introducing a structured deliberative body between the proposal and
execution phases of the governance pipeline will improve the quality,
consistency, and institutional traceability of governance decisions
without altering the constitutional authority structure.

## 2. Context Reference

- FR-20260313-001 (Founder Proposal — AI Assembly Establishment Framework)
- constitution.md v1.3 (Articles 1–10, A–G)
- P-20260312-007 (Proposal Authority Boundary Framework — Phase 2 governance)
- docs/proposal_semantics.md v1.1

## 3. Supporting Observations

- The current governance pipeline moves directly from proposal submission
  to Librarian review. There is no structured deliberation phase between
  proposal generation and institutional review.
- CRC deliberations (CRC-01 through CRC-05) demonstrated that structured
  multi-round deliberation improves proposal quality and reduces revision
  cycles. These were ad hoc mechanisms. An institutionalized equivalent
  would make this quality improvement systematic.
- P-20260312-007 established Phase 2 autonomous governance, which
  increases the volume of proposals processed without Founder intervention.
  A deliberation layer reduces the risk of low-quality proposals reaching
  the execution phase unexamined.

## 4. Proposal Content

### 4.1 Establishment

The AI Assembly is hereby established as a deliberative institution
within the AI Trading OS governance framework.

The AI Assembly functions as a deliberative body, not a decision-making
authority. Final institutional authority remains with the Founder as
defined by the Constitution.

### 4.2 Governance Flow

Upon adoption, the governance pipeline becomes:

```
Proposal
  ↓
AI Assembly Deliberation
  ↓
Librarian Institutional Review
  ↓
Registrar Execution
```

### 4.3 Composition

- Assembly Members: 2
- Appointment Authority: Founder
- The Founder retains the authority to replace members at any time.
- Assembly membership is assigned to AI institutional identities (AiiD).
- AiiD specification will be defined in a subsequent proposal
  (P-20260313-002).

### 4.4 Speaker of the Assembly

The Librarian serves as the Speaker of the Assembly.

Speaker responsibilities:
- maintaining orderly deliberation
- managing agenda flow
- ensuring institutional consistency
- declaring agenda status transitions

The Speaker does not override Founder authority.

### 4.5 Session Types

**Ordinary Session (通常国会)**
- The Founder declares the opening of an Ordinary Session to the
  Librarian.
- Upon receiving the Founder's declaration, the Librarian as Speaker
  updates the Assembly status and the Ordinary Session is opened.
- The Ordinary Session continues until the Founder declares closure
  to the Librarian.
- Upon receiving the closure declaration, the Librarian as Speaker
  updates the Assembly status and the Ordinary Session is closed.
- The Assembly may deliberate multiple proposals during the same
  Ordinary Session.

**Extraordinary Session (臨時国会)**
- Convened by the Librarian when urgent institutional review is required.
- Triggers: institutional inconsistency, system risk, governance anomaly.
- Upon opening: the Ordinary Session is automatically suspended and any
  agenda under deliberation is marked `deliberation_suspended`.
- Continues until closure is declared by the Librarian or Founder.
- Upon closure of an Extraordinary Session, the Assembly enters an idle
  state. The Ordinary Session does not automatically resume. An Ordinary
  Session may only resume when the Founder declares its opening again
  to the Librarian.

**Constitutional Review Council (憲法調査会)**
- Automatically elevated from either session type when an agenda under
  deliberation may result in constitutional modification.

### 4.6 Single-Agenda Rule

Only one agenda may be under deliberation at a time.
A new agenda may not begin until the current agenda is either:
approved, closed, or suspended.

### 4.7 Approval Mechanism

The AI Assembly does not finalize decisions.
Approval occurs when Founder confirmation is recorded.
Upon Founder confirmation, the agenda is considered approved and proceeds
through normal institutional procedures.

### 4.8 Constitutional Amendment Procedure

When operating as a Constitutional Review Council, the Assembly may
deliberate constitutional amendments. Constitutional enactment requires
Founder approval documentation.

### 4.9 Institutional Logging

All Assembly activities must be recorded as trace_events.

Defined event types:
- assembly_opened
- assembly_closed
- extraordinary_session_opened
- agenda_started
- agenda_closed
- agenda_suspended
- constitutional_review_started
- constitutional_amendment_enacted

### 4.10 Relationship to Existing Framework

This proposal does not modify:
- the Constitution
- Founder authority
- the Proposal system
- Librarian review authority
- Registrar execution authority

The AI Assembly introduces a structured deliberation layer within the
existing governance pipeline.

### 4.11 Implementation Requirements

If adopted, the following actions are required:
- trace_event registration of assembly establishment
- documentation updates reflecting the new governance flow
- AiiD specification proposal (P-20260313-002) to follow
