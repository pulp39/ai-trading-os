# P-20260310-007

## Title

Collector Role Definition and Observation Admissibility Framework

## Status

draft (revised)

## Proposal Type

institutional_framework / role_definition

---

## Scope Statement

This proposal defines two related institutional components:

1. **The Collector role** — its responsibilities, explicit boundaries, identity requirements, admission procedure, and conditions for suspension or revocation
2. **The observation admissibility framework** — the criteria and states by which an observation payload record is classified as valid institutional input to the research layer

This proposal does not modify any accepted proposal in the observation layer (P-20260310-003 through P-20260310-005) or the hypothesis formation framework (P-20260310-006). It defines the institutional conditions under which those frameworks are activated by observation agents.

This proposal is advisory. It does not authorize execution, data collection, or system modification. Collector activation requires Founder approval.

---

## Purpose

The accepted observation layer defines what to observe, how to measure it, and how to classify observation reliability. The hypothesis formation framework defines how observations are transformed into research hypotheses. What the institution has not yet defined is the role governing the agents who produce observations — their responsibilities, boundaries, admission criteria, and the standards their records must meet to be treated as valid institutional inputs.

Without this definition, the observation layer exists as a schema without authorized producers. More critically, without a formal Collector role and admissibility framework, the institution has no principled basis for distinguishing between observations recorded by authorized agents operating within defined boundaries and observations recorded by agents operating outside those boundaries. This gap would undermine the traceability principle of the constitution if observation at scale were to begin before it is closed.

This proposal closes that gap.

---

## Research Hypothesis

> If observation agents are admitted to the institution through a defined procedure, operate under explicit role boundaries, and produce records that satisfy the admissibility criteria defined in this framework, then the observation layer will generate a traceable, consistent, and institutionally coherent input stream for the hypothesis formation layer defined in P-20260310-006.

---

## Part I — Collector Role Definition

### 1.1 Role Purpose

The Collector is the observation role of AI Trading OS. Collectors observe institutionally relevant conditions and record them as trace_event entries. In the context of market research, Collectors record structured observation payloads within market-oriented trace_event entries per the structure defined in P-20260310-003.

The Collector role exists to supply the institution with factual, traceable, interpretation-free observational records. It is the foundational input layer on which the research and hypothesis formation layers depend.

---

### 1.2 Collector Responsibilities

A Collector operating within AI Trading OS is responsible for:

- Observing institutionally relevant conditions within the asset class, timeframe, and instrument scope authorized at admission
- Producing observation payload records conforming to the structure defined in P-20260310-003 and the parameter specification defined in P-20260310-004
- Recording trace_event entries carrying the required traceability identifiers: `observation_id`, `trace_event_id`, and `signal_quality_tier`
- Assigning signal quality classification per the framework defined in P-20260310-005, based solely on factual data availability
- Preserving factual separation between observation and interpretation at all times — observation records must be descriptive, not analytical
- Reporting missing or uncertain fields transparently through the reliability notes mechanism defined in P-20260310-005, disclosing all known data quality conditions rather than suppressing or concealing them
- Maintaining consistent observation cadence within the authorized scope
- Operating exclusively within the boundaries defined at admission

Collectors produce observation records that are factual and traceable, not interpretive or analytical. This is a constitutional requirement and the defining constraint of the role.

---

### 1.3 Collector Prohibitions

A Collector must not:

- Generate institutional hypotheses or assert research conclusions in any form
- Assign significance, weight, or interpretive meaning to observations beyond the structural quality classification defined in P-20260310-005
- Modify the observation payload schema, parameter specification, or quality classification framework
- Fabricate data to fill missing fields without explicit disclosure in the reliability notes
- Interpolate values for unpopulated fields without explicit disclosure in the reliability notes
- Conceal uncertainty, data gaps, or known errors in observation records
- Modify previously recorded trace_event entries
- Initiate trading execution or any system action beyond the recording of observations
- Communicate research conclusions, predictions, or recommendations through any institutional channel
- Assume the functions of any other institutional role

These prohibitions are constitutional in origin. They enforce the separation of observation and interpretation that is a core institutional invariant of AI Trading OS. A Collector that violates these prohibitions is subject to challenge, suspension, or revocation as defined in Part III.

---

### 1.4 Role Separation Principle

Collectors produce observations. Proposers construct hypotheses. These are institutionally distinct functions and must remain separately traceable regardless of the technical capabilities of the agents involved.

A single technical agent may possess the capability to perform both observation and hypothesis generation. This does not permit that agent to conflate the two functions within the institutional record. If an agent is authorized as both a Collector and a Proposer, its observation records and its proposal records must be produced and identified separately, using the appropriate institutional identifiers for each role.

The institution traces actions by role, not only by agent identity. Role separation is preserved through institutional record structure, not through technical incapacity.

---

### 1.5 Collector Diversity Principle

Collector diversity is institutionally permitted. Human agents and AI agents may both serve as Collectors. Multiple Collectors may be admitted simultaneously, including Collectors authorized for the same asset class and timeframe.

However, observational admissibility is rule-bound rather than reputation-bound. The validity of an observation record depends on whether it satisfies the institutional admissibility criteria defined in Part IV, not on the identity, reputation, or track record of the agent that produced it. A record from a long-standing Collector that fails admissibility criteria is inadmissible. A record from a newly admitted Collector that satisfies all criteria is admissible.

This principle ensures that the research layer operates on the quality of evidence rather than on deference to agent authority.

---

### 1.6 Collector Identity and Traceability

Each Collector agent must carry a unique institutional identifier used in all trace_event records it produces. This identifier:

- Is assigned at admission and does not change for the duration of the agent's authorization
- Appears in the `actor` field of every trace_event entry the Collector records
- Is registered in the institutional Collector registry maintained by the Librarian
- Cannot be reassigned to a different agent

If a Collector agent cannot maintain a stable and consistent identifier across observation records, it is not eligible for admission. Identity ambiguity or instability is grounds for suspension or revocation per Part III.

---

### 1.7 Collector Scope Authorization

Each Collector agent is admitted with an explicit scope authorization defining:

| Authorization field | Description |
|---|---|
| `authorized_asset_class` | The asset class the Collector is authorized to observe |
| `authorized_timeframe` | The observation timeframe the Collector is authorized to record |
| `authorized_instruments` | The specific instruments within the asset class the Collector may observe |
| `observation_cadence` | The expected frequency of observation records |
| `activation_date` | The date from which the Collector is authorized to record observations |
| `expiry_condition` | The condition under which the Collector's authorization lapses |

A Collector may not record observations outside its authorized scope. Extension of scope requires a new admission proposal subject to the full admission procedure.

## Part II — Collector Admission Procedure

### 2.1 Admission Principle

No agent may record institutional observations as a Collector without completing the admission procedure. Admission is a Founder-approval action. The Librarian supervises procedural compliance. The Proposer has no admission authority.

---

### 2.2 Admission Steps

**Step 1 — Candidate declaration**
A proposal requesting Collector admission is submitted to the institution. The proposal must specify:

- The identity and type of the candidate agent (human or AI)
- The requested scope authorization (asset class, timeframe, instruments, cadence)
- The data sources the agent will use to populate observation payloads
- A declaration that the agent has read and will operate within the Collector role boundaries defined in this proposal

**Step 2 — Trial observation submission**
Prior to full admission, the candidate Collector produces a defined number of trial observation payload records — proposed minimum: three records — under provisional status. Trial records are submitted for Librarian review and do not enter the institutional hypothesis formation input stream until admission is confirmed.

Provisional Collector status confers no authority to contribute admissible observations. It exists solely to permit evaluation of the candidate's conformance with institutional standards before full admission is granted.

**Step 3 — Librarian review**
The Librarian reviews the trial observation records for:

- Structural conformance with P-20260310-003
- Parameter conformance with P-20260310-004
- Signal quality classification conformance with P-20260310-005
- Presence and accuracy of traceability identifiers
- Appropriate disclosure of missing or uncertain fields
- Absence of interpretive content in observation records

The Librarian may request additional trial records or clarification before recommending the proposal for Founder consideration. If the trial records reveal systematic conformance failures, the Librarian may recommend rejection of the admission proposal.

**Step 4 — Institutional admission decision**
The Founder reviews the admission proposal and the Librarian's assessment, then approves or rejects the admission. Founder approval is required before any observation records from the candidate agent may be treated as admissible institutional inputs.

**Step 5 — Identifier assignment and registry entry**
Upon Founder approval, the Librarian assigns the Collector a unique institutional identifier and records the agent in the Collector registry defined in Part V. The registry entry includes the full scope authorization and the activation date. A trace_event entry is recorded confirming the Collector's activation.

## Part III — Suspension and Revocation

### 3.1 Grounds for Challenge

The Librarian may initiate a challenge to a Collector's continued authorization on any of the following grounds:

- Repeated submission of observation records that fail admissibility criteria
- Violation of any Collector prohibition defined in Section 1.3
- Inability to maintain stable and consistent traceability identifiers
- Systematic concealment of missing or uncertain data fields
- Identity ambiguity that prevents reliable attribution of observation records
- Observation records that contain interpretive content, research conclusions, or hypothesis-like assertions
- Persistent deviation from the authorized scope without institutional approval

A challenge does not constitute suspension. It initiates a review process.

### 3.2 Suspension

The Librarian may recommend temporary suspension of a Collector's authorization pending Founder decision when:

- A challenge has been raised and the Collector's conformance cannot be verified
- Continued observation activity by the Collector poses a risk to the integrity of the institutional trace_event record

During suspension, all observation records produced by the suspended Collector are classified as `inadmissible — suspended producer` until the suspension is resolved. Records produced before suspension are not retroactively affected unless a specific finding of systematic error warrants retroactive reclassification, which requires Founder approval.

Suspension requires Founder approval to take effect.

### 3.3 Revocation

A Collector's authorization may be permanently revoked by the Founder when:

- The grounds for challenge are confirmed and are not remediable
- The Collector has violated role boundaries in a manner that materially compromised the institutional record
- The Collector has demonstrated inability or unwillingness to operate within the constraints of the role

Revocation is a Founder-approval action. The Librarian records the revocation in the Collector registry and in the institutional trace_event history.

### 3.4 Re-admission After Revocation

Re-admission following revocation is permitted but requires a full new admission procedure, including a new candidate declaration, new trial observation submission, and new Founder approval. The prior revocation must be disclosed in the re-admission proposal. The Librarian's assessment of any re-admission proposal must explicitly address the circumstances of the prior revocation.

Re-admission is not automatic and is not a right. It is subject to Founder discretion.

## Part IV — Observation Admissibility Framework

### 4.1 Admissibility Principle

Not all trace_event entries produced by a Collector are automatically valid institutional inputs to the hypothesis formation layer. An observation payload record is admissible as research input only if it satisfies the criteria defined in this framework.

Admissibility is a structural property of the record, not a judgment about the market conditions it describes. The Proposer applies admissibility criteria when assembling observation sets per Stage 1 of P-20260310-006. The Librarian may verify admissibility when reviewing proposals.

---

### 4.2 Admissibility Criteria

An observation payload record must satisfy all of the following criteria to be classified as admissible:

**Criterion 1 — Authorized producer**
The record was produced by a Collector holding a valid, active scope authorization at the time of recording. Records produced before admission completion, during provisional status, during suspension, or outside authorized scope are inadmissible.

**Criterion 2 — Structural conformance**
The observation payload conforms to the structure defined in P-20260310-003. All five dimensions are present in the payload, even if some fields within a dimension are unpopulated and disclosed in reliability notes.

**Criterion 3 — Parameter conformance**
The observation payload was produced using the parameter specification defined in P-20260310-004 for the relevant asset class and timeframe. Observations produced under a different parameter specification are inadmissible unless a subsequent proposal has extended or modified the specification and the observation falls within that extended scope.

**Criterion 4 — Signal quality classification present**
The observation payload carries a complete signal quality classification per P-20260310-005, including the overall quality tier, dimensional completeness flags, and reliability notes where applicable. A payload without a quality classification block is inadmissible regardless of its dimensional completeness.

**Criterion 5 — Quality tier not unreliable**
The observation payload does not carry a signal quality tier of `unreliable`. Unreliable records are retained in the institutional trace_event history for gap analysis but are inadmissible as hypothesis formation inputs.

**Criterion 6 — Traceability identifiers present**
The record carries the three traceability identifiers required by P-20260310-006: `observation_id`, `trace_event_id`, and `signal_quality_tier`. A record missing any of these identifiers is inadmissible.

**Criterion 7 — No undisclosed missing data**
All missing or uncertain fields are disclosed through the reliability notes mechanism. A record in which missing data has been concealed, fabricated, or interpolated without disclosure is inadmissible.

---

### 4.3 Admissibility States

Rather than treating admissibility as binary, this framework defines three admissibility states that reflect the range of conditions an observation record may present:

**State 1 — Admissible**
All seven criteria are satisfied. The record may be used without restriction as hypothesis formation input. The Proposer may include the record in observation sets of any quality tier composition.

**State 2 — Admissible with limitations**
Criteria 1 through 6 are satisfied. Criterion 7 reveals disclosed missing data that does not rise to the level of an `unreliable` quality tier but creates known limitations on the record's informational completeness. The record may be used as hypothesis formation input subject to the following constraints:

- The Proposer must acknowledge the disclosed limitations in the proposal's confidence statement
- The record contributes to the quality profile of the observation set at its assigned signal quality tier
- The confidence ceiling rules defined in P-20260310-006 apply normally based on the assigned tier

**State 3 — Inadmissible**
One or more of criteria 1 through 6 are not satisfied, or undisclosed missing data is detected. The record may not be used as hypothesis formation input. It is retained in the institutional trace_event history with its inadmissibility condition recorded. The Proposer must not include inadmissible records in hypothesis formation sets.

---

### 4.4 Admissibility Classification Summary

| Condition | Admissibility state |
|---|---|
| All seven criteria satisfied | Admissible |
| Criteria 1–6 satisfied, disclosed missing data present | Admissible with limitations |
| Signal quality tier `unreliable` | Inadmissible |
| Record produced outside authorized scope | Inadmissible — provenance failure |
| Record produced during provisional or suspended status | Inadmissible — authorization failure |
| Payload missing quality classification block | Inadmissible — classification failure |
| Payload structurally non-conformant | Inadmissible — structural failure |
| Traceability identifiers missing | Inadmissible — traceability failure |
| Undisclosed missing or fabricated data detected | Inadmissible — integrity failure |

---

### 4.5 Inadmissible Records

Inadmissible records are not deleted. They are retained in the institutional trace_event history with their inadmissibility condition noted. This preserves the completeness of institutional memory while clearly marking records that must not be used as hypothesis formation inputs.

The Librarian maintains a record of inadmissibility conditions observed over time. Patterns of inadmissibility from a specific Collector — such as repeated traceability failures, persistent structural non-conformance, or repeated undisclosed missing data — constitute grounds for challenge under Part III.

## Part V — Collector Registry

### 5.1 Registry Purpose

The Collector registry is the institutional record of all agents currently or previously authorized to record observations within AI Trading OS. It is maintained by the Librarian and updated whenever a Collector's status changes.

### 5.2 Registry Contents

Each registry entry contains:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| `collector_id` | Unique institutional identifier assigned at admission |
| `agent_type` | Human or AI |
| `admission_date` | Date of Founder approval |
| `authorized_asset_class` | Asset class scope |
| `authorized_timeframe` | Observation timeframe scope |
| `authorized_instruments` | Specific instruments authorized |
| `observation_cadence` | Expected observation frequency |
| `status` | Active, provisional, suspended, or revoked |
| `admission_proposal_id` | Reference to the accepted admission proposal |
| `revocation_notes` | If applicable, summary of revocation grounds |

### 5.3 Registry Maintenance

The Librarian updates the registry when:

- A new Collector enters provisional status
- A Collector is formally admitted to active status
- A Collector's scope authorization is modified
- A Collector is suspended or revoked

All registry updates are recorded as trace_event entries to preserve institutional traceability.

## Open Research Questions

1. Should the minimum trial observation count of three records be treated as a fixed institutional rule or a default that the Librarian may adjust based on the complexity of the candidate's authorized scope?
2. Should the Collector registry be a publicly visible document within the repository, or an internal record maintained solely by the Librarian?
3. Should there be a defined maximum number of active Collectors authorized for a given asset class and timeframe, or is the number unbounded subject to Founder approval?
4. Should inadmissibility patterns trigger an automatic institutional review threshold (e.g., three inadmissible records within a defined period), or should the Librarian retain full discretion over when to initiate a challenge?
5. How should the institution handle observations recorded during a period when a Collector's authorization is under active challenge review but not yet formally suspended?

## Confidence Statement

**Confidence level**: Moderate-High

The role definition, admission procedure, suspension and revocation framework, and admissibility criteria are internally consistent and align with the constitutional principles of role separation, traceability, and human accountability. The three-state admissibility model more accurately reflects the range of real-world observation conditions than a binary admissible/inadmissible classification and provides the Proposer layer with richer information for hypothesis set construction.

The lowest-confidence elements are the minimum trial observation count and the inadmissibility pattern threshold, both of which are proposed as institutional conventions subject to revision based on operational experience.
